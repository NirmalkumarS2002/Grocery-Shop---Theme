How It Started

A Sunday market table, nine years ago

Stackly's founders set up a single folding table at a weekend market, selling whatever the week's harvest allowed. There was no plan beyond honesty: sell what is ripe, say where it came from, and never let a vegetable sit longer than it has to.

That table is now a small warehouse and a fleet of delivery bikes, but the rule has not changed. Every item on Stackly still passes through hands that know the farm it left.

"Short routes, ripe produce, no shortcuts."

From Field to Basket

How an order reaches your table

Dawn

Picked

Growers harvest only what the day's orders call for, keeping fields producing on a steady rhythm.

Morning

Sorted

Produce is checked by hand for ripeness and quality before it ever reaches a crate.

Midday

Packed

Orders are packed in reusable crates, organized by neighborhood route.

Evening

Delivered

Bikes and small vans carry baskets the last mile, usually within hours of picking.

What Grows Here

The standards we don't bend on

01

Source Transparency

Every product page names the farm it came from, not just a region or a brand.

02

Fair Pay to Growers

Farms are paid a fixed share of every sale, agreed before the season starts, not negotiated after harvest.

03

Low-Waste Packing

Crates are reused on every route, and unsold produce goes to neighborhood food programs the same day.

04

Same-Day Picking

Nothing sits in cold storage longer than overnight before it reaches a doorstep.
